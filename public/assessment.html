Think about the last 2 weeks and rate how often each concern was seen at home, in school, during routines, or in social settings.

How to answer

Use one consistent scale for every question

Each question measures concern level. A score of 1 means the issue was not really observed. A score of 5 means it appeared often and had a strong impact.

1 Never / not observed 2 Rarely 3 Sometimes 4 Often 5 Nearly every day / severe

Notice sadness, flat mood, or reduced enjoyment in usual play and activities.

Think about excessive worry, strong fear, avoidance, or needing unusual reassurance.

Rate anger, reactivity, low frustration tolerance, or intense emotional escalation.

Include trouble sleeping, frequent waking, oversleeping, or low daytime energy.

Consider schoolwork, daily routines, listening, task completion, and mental restlessness.

Notice reduced play, avoiding peers, staying alone more, or pulling away from usual activities.

This question helps the score become more practical by measuring whether concerns are affecting routines, learning, participation, or relationships.
