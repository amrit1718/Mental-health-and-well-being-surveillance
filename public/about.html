About the project

Building a safer way to observe and support child well-being

CalmSteps is designed as a practical prototype for early detection and progress monitoring in school and home environments.

Project Purpose

The system helps caregivers collect regular observations about a child's emotional and behavioral health. It turns those observations into clear scores, risk levels, and visual trends so support can happen earlier.

The goal is not to label children, but to create a gentle, structured way to notice changes in mood, worry, sleep, concentration, social engagement, and day-to-day functioning.

Who can use it?

Parents Teachers School counselors Community caregivers

The design keeps the workflow simple enough for non-technical users while still supporting meaningful monitoring and record keeping.

System objectives

Main goals of the platform

Observe

Capture structured well-being data through a guided assessment form.

Assess

Calculate a simple score and categorize results into low, moderate, or high risk.

Track

Visualize progress over time to support consistent check-ins and timely care.

Suggested real-world flow

Week 1

Create the caregiver account and add the child's profile.

Weekly

Complete a short mental health assessment and monitor score changes.

When risk rises

Receive a system alert, review the dashboard, and consider professional follow-up.
